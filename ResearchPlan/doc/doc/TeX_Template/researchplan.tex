\section*{Background}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{\protect\numberline{}Background}%
Earth's systems are changing at an increasing rate \citep{Murphy2009,IPCC2019SpecialReport}. A geographically constrained ocean and cryosphere rate of change is being assessed in this dissertation. To understand this, and the linkages of this work, to the global climate systems, I provide a brief background on the subject matter.

It is well established knowledge that the ocean can be thought of as a quasi-storage mechanism of
atmospheric energy, in the form of heat, and it is therefore a key component of global heat content
in Earth's climate systems \citep{Orsi1995,Levitus2005,Bindoff2007,Purkey2010}. Our current
capability to constrain the heat budget in the deep ocean via global climate models is in need of refinement \citep{Domingues2008,Murphy2009,Purkey2012,Durack2018}. As a result, at present, the uncertainty in deep-ocean heat uptake has been shown to be the most significant cause of variation among climate models \citep{Boe2009,Durack2018}.

Coastal ocean polynyas (\textit{latent-heat polynyas}) of Antarctica have been shown to be areas of significant heat flux, through their association with sea ice production (\sip) and dense shelf water (\dsw) formation \citep{Massom1999,Maqueda2004,Williams2008,Ohshima2013,Nakayama2014,Ohshima2016,Williams2016,Kusahara2017b,Naughten2017}. It is this water mass modification that is a key driver of the thermohaline circulation \citep{Gawarkiewicz1995,Massom2001}. The distribution of landfast sea ice (\fastice) is directly correlated to \textit{latent-heat polynyas} \citep{Massom2001,Fraser2012,Ohshima2013,Kusahara2017a}. Work done recently by Fraser et. al, has shown the the interannual variability, spatial pattern and frequency of \fastice distribution around Antarctica, and now, with considerable effort, a growing \fastice database of Antartica exists \citet{Fraser2020}.

\Fastice (\textit{and iceberg distribution?}) ignorance in coupled ice-ocean numerical models reproduces \dsw incorrectly or not at all \citep{Ohshima2013,Nakayama2014,Liu2017,Fraser2019}.
